# ketamine × serotonergic_opioids

Ketamine plus serotonergic opioids is best treated as a caution interaction, driven mainly by additive CNS/respiratory depression and indirect serotonin-syndrome risk in poly-serotonergic stacks rather than a standalone direct serotonin-toxic signal from ketamine.

## Dataset-ready interaction entry

- Danger: Amber
- Confidence: Medium

## Evidence-based interaction readout
- Classification: Amber / caution.
- Not clearly contraindicated in all supervised contexts.

## Core interpretation
Ketamine is primarily an NMDA antagonist and is not a typical serotonin-syndrome culprit. Serotonergic opioids can contribute serotonergic load in poly-serotonergic stacks, while additive CNS and respiratory depression remains the main practical risk driver. Clinical co-use in perioperative and postoperative settings supports caution framing instead of blanket contraindication.

## Action posture
Avoid unsupervised or recreational high-dose co-use. Avoid combining with other depressants, and strongly avoid stacking multiple serotonergic agents. In clinical settings monitor consciousness, respiratory rate, oxygen saturation, blood pressure, heart rate, and temperature. Seek urgent care for serotonin-syndrome signs or respiratory depression.

Monitor during and after overlapping effects because respiratory depression may emerge as sedation deepens.

## Evidence confidence
Overall: MEDIUM. Direct clinical data specifically on ketamine plus serotonergic opioids causing serotonin syndrome are very limited; risk estimate is extrapolated from serotonergic opioid-antidepressant literature and general opioid-sedative harm-reduction principles.
