## Summary
This systematic review evaluates drug–drug interactions involving classic psychedelics, including LSD, psilocybin, mescaline, DMT, and ayahuasca. It identifies pharmacokinetic and pharmacodynamic interactions across multiple drug classes, including antidepressants, antipsychotics, and MAOIs. Findings show variable outcomes, including potentiation, attenuation, or no effect. Serious adverse events were rare, but evidence remains limited and heterogeneous.

## Mechanisms Described
- 5-HT2A receptor agonism
- CYP450-mediated metabolism
- MAO-mediated metabolism
- Receptor competition (pharmacodynamic)
- Transporter interactions (e.g. P-gp)

---

## Clinical / Practical Guidance
- Avoid combining psychedelics with strong serotonergic medications without supervision
- Expect attenuation of effects with SSRIs or antipsychotics
- Monitor cardiovascular and psychological effects

---

## Contraindications / Risks
- Potential serotonin toxicity
- Altered drug metabolism
- Unpredictable pharmacodynamic effects
- Limited safety data in polypharmacy

---

## Interaction Relevance
- Psychedelics + antidepressants → attenuation or modulation
- Psychedelics + antipsychotics → receptor-level antagonism
- DMT + MAOI → pharmacokinetic activation
- Psychedelics + CYP inhibitors → altered metabolism

---

## Limitations
- Limited number of human studies
- High heterogeneity
- Reliance on case reports
- Lack of controlled DDI trials
