## Summary

This review provides a detailed pharmacological and clinical analysis of serotonin toxicity (serotonin syndrome), with particular focus on interactions between monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs) and opioid analgesics. It establishes that **serotonin toxicity is a dose-dependent toxidrome resulting from elevated intra-synaptic serotonin**, and that **combinations of serotonergic agents acting via different mechanisms—especially MAOIs combined with serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SRIs)—are the primary cause of severe and fatal cases**  [oai_citation:0‡Gillman-2005-Monoamine oxidase inhibitors opioid analgesics and serotonin toxicity.pdf](sediment://file_000000005554720aa8cc96ed7a5ac052)  

The paper identifies specific opioid analgesics with serotonergic properties and distinguishes them from those without such effects, providing a strong mechanistic basis for predicting drug–drug interactions.

## Mechanisms Described

- Monoamine oxidase inhibition → decreased serotonin breakdown  
- Serotonin reuptake inhibition → increased synaptic serotonin  
- Serotonin release (in some agents) → further elevation of serotonin  
- Synergistic pharmacodynamic interaction between MAOIs and SRIs  
- Dose-dependent accumulation of serotonin leading to toxicity  

---

## Clinical / Practical Guidance

- Avoid combining MAOIs with serotonergic opioids (especially meperidine, tramadol, methadone, dextromethorphan)
- Avoid combining MAOIs with SSRIs, SNRIs, or other SRIs
- Prefer non-serotonergic opioids (morphine, oxycodone, etc.) when analgesia is required
- Recognize early signs of serotonin toxicity (clonus, hyperreflexia, agitation)
- Treat severe cases with 5-HT2A antagonists and supportive care  [oai_citation:1‡Gillman-2005-Monoamine oxidase inhibitors opioid analgesics and serotonin toxicity.pdf](sediment://file_000000005554720aa8cc96ed7a5ac052)  

---

## Contraindications / Risks

- MAOI + SRI combinations → high risk of severe serotonin toxicity
- MAOI + serotonergic opioids → potentially fatal interactions
- MAOI + serotonin releasers (e.g. MDMA) → extreme risk
- Rapid onset of toxicity following combination exposure
- Dose-dependent escalation to life-threatening hyperthermia and rigidity
