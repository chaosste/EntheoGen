## Summary
This systematic review examines the pharmacological interactions underlying ayahuasca’s psychoactive effects. The primary mechanism involves β-carboline MAO-A inhibition enabling oral DMT bioavailability. However, the review highlights that current understanding of interactions between DMT and harmala alkaloids is incomplete, with unclear evidence regarding whether effects are additive or synergistic. Additional serotonergic, neuroendocrine, and potential neuroplastic effects suggest more complex multi-compound interactions.

## Mechanisms Described
- MAO-A inhibition (pharmacokinetic interaction)
- Serotonergic receptor agonism (5-HT2A, 5-HT1A)
- Serotonin reuptake inhibition (THH)
- Sympathomimetic activation
- Potential neuroplasticity (BDNF increase)

---

## Clinical / Practical Guidance
- Avoid combining MAOIs with serotonergic drugs due to interaction risk
- Monitor cardiovascular effects during use
- Recognise variability in composition and dosing

---

## Contraindications / Risks
- Hypertensive effects
- Potential serotonin toxicity with serotonergic drugs
- CNS overstimulation
- Limited safety data for poly-drug use

---

## Interaction Relevance
- DMT + MAOI → bioavailability enabling interaction
- DMT + serotonergic drugs → potential serotonergic risk
- Ayahuasca + antidepressants → pharmacodynamic interaction risk

---

## Limitations
- Small number of studies (n=16)
- Heterogeneous methodologies
- Lack of controlled interaction studies
- Limited clinical data

---

## Notes
Key foundational paper for mechanistic inference. Strong support for pharmacokinetic interaction model but weak clarity on synergy vs additivity.
