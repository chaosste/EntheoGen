## Summary
This presentation outlines pharmacological mechanisms and clinical risks associated with ayahuasca use, focusing on MAO-A inhibition, serotonergic toxicity, and hypertensive crisis. It provides practical harm-reduction guidance on drug and dietary interactions. While not primary research, it synthesizes pharmacological principles and clinical experience into actionable recommendations.

## Mechanisms Described
- Reversible MAO-A inhibition
- Serotonergic toxicity (excess 5-HT signaling)
- Tyramine-induced hypertensive response
- Sympathomimetic cardiovascular effects
- Pharmacokinetic washout (half-life dependent)

---

## Clinical / Practical Guidance
- Avoid serotonergic drugs prior to ayahuasca
- Avoid tyramine-rich foods when MAO inhibition is present
- Use ≥5 half-life rule for medication washout  [oai_citation:0‡Benjamin-Malcolm-PharmD-MPH-2023-Ayahuasca and Drug Interaction.pdf](sediment://file_00000000e0d871f4b71d3c3ac377b796)
- Screen for psychiatric risk (e.g. psychosis, bipolar disorder)
- Monitor cardiovascular responses during use

## Interaction Relevance
- MAOI + SSRIs → serotonin syndrome risk
- MAOI + stimulants → hypertensive risk
- Ayahuasca + antidepressants → contraindicated
- Ayahuasca + tyramine → hypertensive crisis
- Psychedelics + antipsychotics → attenuation
